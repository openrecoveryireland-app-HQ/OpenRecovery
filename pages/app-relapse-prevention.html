Relapse Prevention

Relapse prevention is not about waiting until everything feels dangerous. It is about noticing pressure early, understanding your warning signs, and taking one safe step before things build up.

Visit Main Website

What makes me more vulnerable?

Some days are harder than others. Relapse risk can build when pressure, emotion, routine, tiredness, isolation, or old habits start coming together.

People

Are there people who make recovery harder for me today? This might include drinking friends, people I feel unsafe around, people I argue with, or people connected to old routines.

Places

Are there places I need to avoid or be careful around? Pubs, off-licences, certain houses, payday routes, parties, or anywhere strongly connected to drinking or using can be high-risk.

Feelings

Am I angry, lonely, anxious, bored, ashamed, overwhelmed, resentful, or feeling sorry for myself? Strong feelings can become dangerous when I keep them hidden.

Routine

Have I stopped doing the simple things that help me? Sleep, food, meetings, contact, exercise, structure, honesty, medication, appointments, prayer, meditation, or journaling may all matter.

My warning signs

Warning signs are not failures. They are signals. The earlier I notice them, the more choices I still have.

Area Possible warning signs
Thinking “I’m grand now”, “I can handle one”, “Nobody understands”, “What’s the point?”
Feelings Restless, angry, lonely, anxious, low, ashamed, bored, numb, or overwhelmed.
Behaviour Isolating, lying, cancelling plans, skipping support, hiding stress, or testing old situations.
Recovery Not using supports, avoiding honesty, stopping healthy routines, or drifting from what was helping.

What can I do before it gets worse?

Move away from risk

If I am near alcohol, drugs, conflict, or a risky situation, the first step may be physical: leave the place, change direction, go outside, or get around safer people.

Tell the truth earlier

I do not have to explain everything perfectly. A simple honest message can be enough: “I’m not in a great place today and I need to stay connected.”

Use the app before the urge peaks

Open Mosaic before things feel unmanageable. Use Hold the Line, write in the journal, read a reflection, take a quiet moment, or look at your saved supports.

Lower the pressure

Eat something, drink water, rest if possible, take a walk, shower, pause an argument, delay a decision, or get through the next ten minutes without making things worse.

Quick relapse prevention check-in

Pick one question. You do not have to answer them all.

What is the risk today?

Is it a person, place, feeling, thought, memory, craving, argument, money, free time, tiredness, or something else?

What am I telling myself?

Is the thought helping my recovery, or is it giving me permission to move closer to danger?

What has worked before?

Think of one action that helped in the past. It does not have to be dramatic. It just has to move you in the right direction.

What is the next safe step?

Make it small and clear: leave, message someone, eat, walk, journal, use Hold the Line, go home, delay, or ask for help.

If a setback has happened

A setback does not have to become a full return to old patterns. The next decision still matters.

Stop the slide

Get away from the risky situation if possible. Do not turn one setback into permission to give up on yourself.

Get honest quickly

Tell one safe person or professional support as soon as you can. Shame grows in silence. Recovery starts moving again when the truth is back in the open.

Look for the lesson

What led up to it? What was I feeling? What did I ignore? What could I do differently next time? This is not about beating yourself up. It is about learning the warning signs.

Return to support

Use your recovery supports, contact a professional service if needed, and make the next right decision. You do not have to wait until things are worse.

Simple prevention plan

Use these questions as prompts. You can answer them in your Mosaic journal, on paper, or with someone you trust.

Question My answer
What is my main risk today?
