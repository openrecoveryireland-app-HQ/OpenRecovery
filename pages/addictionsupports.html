Addiction Supports

Start with what sounds closest to what you need, or browse the addiction support lists.

Not sure where to start?

Choose the option that sounds closest. You do not need to know the name of a service.

I need a meeting Peer support meetings such as AA, NA, LifeRing, SMART Recovery, Al-Anon, and Gamblers Anonymous. I need to talk to someone or get advice Helplines, information services, addiction counsellor directories, and service finders. I think I need treatment Residential treatment centres and structured addiction treatment services. I need community addiction support Harm reduction, outreach, rehabilitation supports, local HSE addiction services, and community projects. I need counselling or professional support Addiction counsellor directories, professional contacts, support services, and referral information. I am worried about someone else Family and peer-support options, including Al-Anon and other support routes for people affected by someone else’s addiction.

Browse Addiction Support Lists

You can also browse each addiction support list directly.

Peer Support Meetings Recovery meetings and peer-led support groups, including Alcoholics Anonymous, Narcotics Anonymous, LifeRing Ireland, SMART Recovery Ireland, Al-Anon, and Gamblers Anonymous. Helplines, Directories & Professional Support Addiction helplines, counselling directories, service finders, and professional support options for people looking for advice, information, or someone to contact. Residential Treatment Centres Residential addiction treatment centres and structured treatment services. These are listed neutrally, without ranking one centre over another. Community Addiction Supports Community-based addiction supports, harm reduction services, outreach, rehabilitation supports, housing-related addiction supports, and local HSE addiction services.

Important Note

OpenRecovery does not provide emergency services, medical advice, diagnosis, counselling, or treatment. If you or someone else is in immediate danger, contact emergency services.

Service information can change. Always check directly with the service before making decisions about support, treatment, appointments, or access.
