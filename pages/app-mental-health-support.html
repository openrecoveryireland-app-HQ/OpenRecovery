Mental Health Support

Recovery and mental health are closely connected. Stress, anxiety, low mood, shame, anger, loneliness, poor sleep, or feeling overwhelmed can all make the day harder. This page is here to help you notice what is going on and take one safe next step.

Visit Main Website

What am I dealing with today?

Anxiety or panic

Anxiety can make your body feel under threat even when you are trying to stay safe. Slow things down. Breathe, sit down if you need to, drink water, and try not to make big decisions while the feeling is at its strongest.

Low mood

Low mood can make everything feel heavy. You may feel tired, flat, guilty, irritated, hopeless, or disconnected. A small action still counts: shower, food, fresh air, a message, a journal note, or asking for support.

Overwhelm

If everything feels too much, reduce the size of the problem. You do not have to fix your whole life today. Focus on the next ten minutes, the next safe choice, or the next thing that helps you stay steady.

Anger, shame, or regret

Strong feelings can push people towards old patterns. Try not to act straight away. Pause, move away from conflict if possible, and write down what you are feeling before you respond.

Loneliness or isolation

Isolation can make thoughts louder. One small connection can help: a message, a call, sitting near people, going outside, or using one of the support options already saved in the app.

Quick mental health check-in

Pick one question. You can answer in your Mosaic journal, on paper, or with someone you trust.

Question My answer
What feeling is strongest right now?  
What might have triggered this feeling?  
What is one thing I can do to lower the pressure?  
Do I need to contact someone?  
Do I feel safe right now?  

Small steps that may help

Ground yourself

Look around and name five things you can see. Put your feet on the floor. Notice your breathing. Remind yourself: “This is a feeling. I do not have to act on it straight away.”

Check the basics

Tiredness, hunger, dehydration, too much caffeine, poor sleep, or being alone too long can all make mental health feel worse. Start with the body if the mind feels too busy.

Use the app early

Open Mosaic before things feel unmanageable. Use Hold the Line, a reflection, meditation, your journal, or a saved support option.

Make the day smaller

Do not try to solve everything at once. Pick one safe action: wash, eat, walk, rest, tidy one thing, send one message, or write one honest sentence.

When practical problems are driving this

Money, housing, work, study, appointments, forms, transport, or family pressure can all affect mental health. If the main problem today is practical, use the Practical Help page.

Irish mental health support options

HSE Your Mental Health information line

This is a 24/7 information line for mental health supports and services in Ireland. It is not a counselling service, but it can help you find out what supports are available and how to access them.

Freephone: 1800 111 888

Visit Website

Samaritans Ireland

Samaritans offers confidential emotional support if you are struggling to cope or need someone to talk to.

Freephone: 116 123
Email: jo@samaritans.ie

Visit Website

Text About It

Text About It is a free, anonymous 24/7 text support service for mental health and emotional wellbeing.

Free-text: HELLO to 50808

Visit Website

Pieta

Pieta provides crisis support for people experiencing suicidal thoughts, self-harm, or bereavement by suicide.

Freephone: 1800 247 247
Text: HELP to 51444

Visit Website

Aware

Aware provides support and information for people affected by anxiety, depression, bipolar disorder, and mood-related difficulties.

Freephone: 1800 80 48 48
Monday to Sunday: 10am to 10pm

Visit Website

If things feel urgent or unsafe

If you or someone else is about to be harmed, call emergency services on 112 or 999, or go to the nearest emergency department.

If you are not in immediate danger but you feel unable to cope, use your Safety Plan and contact support now. Do not wait until things get worse.
