Practical Help

Practical problems can put real pressure on recovery. Money, housing, work, forms, appointments, transport, food, or daily routine can all feel too much when they build up. This page is here to help you slow things down and choose one practical next step.

Visit Main Website

What practical pressure am I dealing with?

Money, bills, or debt

Money stress can feel frightening and embarrassing, but hiding it often makes it heavier. Start by naming the problem: rent, bills, debt, food, fines, gambling debt, loans, or not knowing what is coming in and going out.

Housing or tenancy problems

Housing pressure can affect sleep, safety, mental health, and recovery. If there is a risk of losing accommodation, rent arrears, landlord pressure, unsafe housing, or nowhere to stay, get advice early.

Work, job search, or training

Looking for work can be stressful, especially in recovery. Keep the first step small: update a CV, look at one job, contact Intreo, ask about training, or make a simple weekly plan.

Forms, payments, or appointments

Forms and appointments can pile up quickly. Break them down. What is due first? What letter needs answering? What appointment needs booking? What document is missing?

Daily routine

Recovery can be helped by ordinary structure: getting up, washing, eating, leaving the house, making one call, keeping one appointment, and sleeping at a reasonable time.

Quick practical check-in

Pick one question. You can answer in your Mosaic journal, on paper, or with someone you trust.

Question My answer
What practical problem is putting the most pressure on me today?  
Is this urgent, important, or something that can wait?  
What is the smallest next step I can take?  
Who or what service could help with this?  
What do I need to do before the end of today?  

Small practical steps

Timeframe Small step
Right now Write down the problem in one sentence.
Today Find one phone number, email address, form, bill, letter, or appointment detail.
Today Make one call, send one email, or ask one person for help.
Next 24 hours Put the next appointment, deadline, or task into your calendar.
This week Contact the right support service instead of trying to carry it alone.

Work, jobs, and routine

Keep it realistic

Work can be part of rebuilding life, but it does not have to be solved in one day. The first step might be updating a CV, looking at one course, asking about training, or speaking to Intreo.

Look for structure, not pressure

Sometimes the immediate need is routine rather than a full-time job straight away. Volunteering, training, part-time work, study, appointments, or a weekly plan can all help rebuild confidence.

Notice recovery risks

Some jobs, hours, people, or environments may put pressure on recovery. It is okay to think about what kind of work is safe, realistic, and suitable for where you are now.

Irish practical support options

Citizens Information

Citizens Information provides information on rights, entitlements and public services in Ireland, including social welfare, housing, employment rights, education, health, tax and consumer issues.

Phone: 0818 07 4000
Monday to Friday: 9am to 8pm

Visit Website

MABS

MABS gives free, confidential support with money, budgeting and debt. This may be useful if bills, rent, loans, arrears, gambling debt, or money stress are putting pressure on recovery.

Phone: 0818 07 2000
Monday to Friday: 9am to 8pm

Visit Website

Intreo

Intreo is Ireland’s public employment service and a point of contact for employment and income supports. It can help with jobseeker supports, employment services and training options.

Visit Website

JobsIreland

JobsIreland is an online recruitment service for jobseekers and employers. It is part of Intreo and can be used to search for jobs and employment supports.

Visit Website

Threshold

Threshold provides free and confidential advice for renters with housing problems. This may be useful for tenancy problems, rent pressure, notices, deposits, or risk of homelessness.

Freephone: 1800 454 454
Monday to Friday: 9am to 9pm

Visit Website

Additional Needs Payment

The Additional Needs Payment may help with essential expenses that cannot be paid from weekly income or other resources. It may be available even if a person is working and on a low income.

Visit Website

Homeless services through local authorities

Local authorities provide and fund emergency and temporary accommodation for people experiencing homelessness. If housing feels urgent, contact your local authority housing or homeless services as early as possible.

When practical pressure affects recovery

Practical pressure can increase stress, isolation, anger, shame, anxiety, and relapse risk. If this is starting to affect your recovery or mental health, use the related support pages below.
