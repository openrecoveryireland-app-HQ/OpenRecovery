Recovery Support

Recovery is not something you have to work out all at once. This page is here to help you slow things down, think about what kind of support you need, and take one small step that helps you stay connected.

Visit Main Website

What kind of support do I need today?

I need to steady myself

Start small. Step away from pressure if you can. Drink water, eat something, take a few slow breaths, or use a calming part of the Mosaic app such as Hold the Line, reflections, meditation, or your journal.

I need to understand what is going on

Try to name what is happening without judging yourself. Are you tired, lonely, angry, anxious, bored, overwhelmed, ashamed, or under pressure? Naming it can make the next step clearer.

I need help staying connected

Isolation can make recovery harder. A small connection may be enough for today. That could mean sending one honest message, making one phone call, writing in your journal, or using a support option already saved in the app.

I need professional support

Professional support in Ireland can include a GP, counselling service, community alcohol and drug service, or a residential treatment programme. You do not have to know the perfect option before asking for guidance.

Quick recovery check-in

You do not have to answer everything. Pick one question that fits today.

What is happening right now?

Try writing one honest sentence in your journal. No performance. No perfect wording. Just the truth of the moment.

What could make today difficult?

Think about people, places, feelings, routines, money, stress, free time, arguments, tiredness, or anything else that could put pressure on your recovery.

What has helped me before?

Recovery often leaves clues. Think of one thing that helped before, even if it was small: a walk, a meeting, a message, a shower, food, sleep, music, prayer, meditation, or getting away from a risky situation.

What is one safe action I can take now?

Keep it small enough to do. One message. One glass of water. One journal note. One walk. One page. One phone call. One honest decision.

Small recovery steps

Timeframe Small step
Right now Pause, breathe, drink water, and move away from anything risky if possible.
Today Use one part of the app that helps you stay steady.
Today Write down what is going on instead of carrying it all in your head.
Next 24 hours Reach out for support before things build up too much.
This week Look at whether you need extra support from a GP, counsellor, aftercare service, or local alcohol and drug service.

Irish recovery support options

HSE Drugs and Alcohol Helpline

The HSE Drugs and Alcohol Helpline offers confidential information, support, guidance and referral for drug and alcohol concerns in Ireland.

Freephone: 1800 459 459
Email: helpline@hse.ie
Monday to Friday: 9.30am to 5.30pm

Find local alcohol and drug services

Drugs.ie has a national services directory where people can search for alcohol and drug services in Ireland, including support, counselling, medical support, aftercare, residential and non-residential services.

Aftercare and staying connected

Aftercare can include continuing recovery support, peer support, relapse prevention counselling, therapeutic activities, and help staying connected after treatment or during ongoing recovery.
